# Data Sources

Where the default values and slider ranges come from. All research was conducted and verified in March 2026, targeting a Berlin new-build cooperative project with construction starting ~2027.

## Construction Costs (KG 300+400)

**Default: €2,800/m² BGF · Median: €2,800–3,100/m² BGF · Range (slider): €2,000–€4,200**

### Verified Findings

Multiple sources confirm that MFH construction costs (KG 300+400) in 2026 lie between **€2,800–3,800/m²** depending on region, standard, and building method. These figures are mostly quoted per m² Wohnfläche (living area); when converted to BGF using a typical 1.30 ratio, costs per m² BGF are ~25% lower.

- **Stadtblick Architekten** (Hamburg/Berlin) reports KG 300+400 at **€3,900–4,000/m² Mietfläche** for MFH. With a typical BGF-to-Mietfläche ratio, this translates to approximately **€2,800–3,100/m² BGF**. They recommend a **3–5% surcharge** for 2027 construction start on their 2026 reference values.
- **Pro Bauherr** (MFH cost calculator): MFH costs in 2026 between **€2,800–3,800/m²**, with sub-€3,000/m² achievable through cost-optimized planning.
- **Baufrage.net**: MFH mid-range at **€1,900–2,400/m²** (appears to be per Wohnfläche, simpler standard).
- **Statistisches Bundesamt** (via a-better-place.de): Construction costs rose ~3.1% in 2025 vs. prior year. For 2026, price increases have moderated to ~2.5–3.1% (BBSR forecast Oct 2025).
- One source (Ziegler Haus) notes MFH costs are **20–30% below EFH costs** due to shared infrastructure.
- **KfW-40 premium**: achieving KfW-40 standard adds approximately **€300–500/m²** to base costs (Ziegler Haus).

### Assessment

The original default of **€2,800/m² BGF** remains a reasonable central estimate for a cooperative build with good (but not luxury) fitout and KfW-40 standard. The range should be widened slightly to allow for testing extreme values.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Lower bound (economical, EH55) | €2,200/m² BGF |
| Median expected value | €2,900/m² BGF |
| Upper bound (EH40-QNG, higher spec) | €3,500/m² BGF |
| **Slider range** | **€2,000–€4,200/m² BGF** |

## Bodenrichtwert (Land Values)

**Default: €400/m² · Median: €400–600/m² · Range (slider): €100–€2,500**

### Verified Findings

- **Berlin-wide average Bodenrichtwert** (Stichtag 01.01.2025): **€1,308/m²**, down 3.5% from prior year (€1,356/m²). This average is heavily skewed by central districts and is not representative for cooperative land acquisition.
- **Highest**: Mitte at ~€6,926/m²; Tiergarten at ~€5,723/m²; Charlottenburg at ~€5,405/m².
- **Lowest**: Blankenfelde (Pankow) at **€256/m²**.
- **Outer/middle-ring zones** relevant for cooperative projects:
  - Marzahn-Hellersdorf: ~€250–450/m²
  - Treptow-Köpenick outer areas: ~€300–500/m²
  - Lichtenberg outskirts: ~€400–700/m²
  - Pankow outskirts: ~€400–800/m²
- **Actual sales prices** in 2024 ran approximately **9% below Bodenrichtwert** on average (Gutachterausschuss ad-hoc report Feb 2025 for Bauland im individuellen Wohnungsbau). This ratio varies by district.
- The **Bodenrichtwert 2026** (Stichtag 01.01.2026) was expected to be published between March–June 2026 and was not yet available at time of research.
- The Gutachterausschuss reduced Bodenrichtwerte in 2025 by **up to 5% in inner-city locations** and **up to 10% in peripheral locations** vs. prior year.

### Assessment

The original default of **€400/m²** is well-calibrated for outer/middle-ring sites where a cooperative could realistically acquire land (either via Konzeptverfahren/Erbbaurecht or purchase). No correction needed.

| Upper bound (inner residential, e.g. Lichtenberg/Pankow core) | €1,000/m² |
| **Slider range** | **€100–€2,500/m²** |

## Architecture & Planning (HOAI)

**Default: 15% · Median: 13%–15% · Range (slider): 10%–22%**

### Verified Findings

- Since the **2021 EuGH ruling / HOAI reform** (effective 01.01.2021), the HOAI fee tables serve only as **orientation values** (Orientierungswerte), not binding min/max. Architects are free to set fees.
- A **Mehrfamilienhaus** typically falls in **Honorarzone III** (durchschnittliche Planungsanforderungen). More complex MFH designs with multiple functions or demanding requirements may qualify for **Honorarzone IV**.
- For a full-service engagement (all 9 Leistungsphasen, LPH 1–9), the HOAI orientation suggests roughly **12–16% of anrechenbare Kosten** (KG 300+400) for Honorarzone III at mid-range Honorarsatz.
- Specific example from HOAI §35: For €300,000 netto construction costs (small project), the Honorarzone III range is €39,981–€49,864 netto (~13.3%–16.6%). At higher construction costs typical for MFH (€2–5M+), the percentage decreases due to the degressive HOAI fee curve — typically landing at **11–14%** for larger projects.
- **LPH 8 (Bauüberwachung)** alone represents **32%** of the total fee, confirming that full-service including Bauüberwachung significantly increases total cost.
- For cooperative projects with participatory design processes (Mitbestimmung), additional coordination effort may warrant **15–18%**.
- The original document's note about Baunebenkosten being **10–15% of Gesamtsumme** (from wohnen-und-finanzieren.de) aligns with a 15% architecture estimate when other planning disciplines (structural engineer, building services) are included.

### Assessment

The default of **15%** is a sound estimate for a cooperative MFH project with full HOAI scope (LPH 1–9) including Bauüberwachung, especially when participatory design adds coordination overhead. For larger projects with cost-optimized planning, 12–13% is achievable. The range has been slightly widened.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Lower bound (large project, LPH 1–7 only) | 10% |
| Median expected value (full scope, HZ III) | 14% |
| Upper bound (complex cooperative, HZ IV, participatory) | 20% |
| **Slider range** | **€10%–22%** |

### Sources
- HOAI §35 Honorartafel: https://dejure.org/gesetze/HOAI/35.html
- HOAI.de Rechner: https://www.hoai.de/hoai/rechner/
- HOAI Honorartafeln: https://www.hoai.de/hoai/honorartafeln/
- bauen.de HOAI explainer: https://www.bauen.de/a/die-hoai-und-ihre-leistungsphasen-so-berechnet-der-architekt-sein-honorar/

## Energieberater / QNG Certification

**Default: €25,000 (for EH40+QNG, ~10–20 WE MFH) · Median: €20,000–35,000 · Range (slider): €5,000–€50,000**

### Verified Findings

The costs comprise two components: (a) Energieberatung/Effizienzhaus planning, and (b) QNG certification (auditor + Zertifizierungsstelle fees).

**QNG certification costs alone:**
- BiRN (Bau-Institut für Ressourceneffizientes und Nachhaltiges Bauen) publishes standardized fees: EFH (1 WE) = €559 netto; ZFH = €759 netto; 3–5 WE = roughly €960–1,360 netto; 6+ WE starts at ~€6,000.
- Prof. Dr.-Ing. Natalie Essig (BiRN founder) states QNG certification costs typically **€5,000–10,000** per project.
- n12-ingenieure.de: QNG for MFH (6 WE+) starts at **€6,000** for the certification proper.
- Auditor/Nachhaltigkeitsberater honorar is negotiated individually and is typically a larger portion.

**Combined Energieberatung + QNG for MFH:**
- **DWE-Beratung** (Energieberater Sebastian Möhrer): 
  - 3 WE with QNG: **€20,000–30,000**
  - 5 WE with QNG: **€30,000–40,000**
  - 10 WE with QNG: shown as significantly higher, with substantial KfW credit advantage.
- nachhaltiges-zuhause.de: QNG-Siegel costs **€6,000–15,000** for certification (EFH to MFH), not including the energy consulting itself.
- For a 10–20 WE MFH with EH40+QNG: total consulting + certification costs plausibly **€25,000–45,000**.

**EH40 without QNG:**
- Much cheaper: energy consulting for MFH alone ~€8,000–15,000, no QNG auditor needed.

### Assessment

Original estimates were reasonable. Default raised slightly to €25,000 as a better midpoint for a 10–20 WE project with QNG.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Lower bound (EH40 without QNG, small MFH) | €8,000 |
| Median expected value (EH40+QNG, 10–15 WE) | €25,000 |
| Upper bound (EH40+QNG-PREMIUM, 20+ WE) | €45,000 |
| **Slider range** | **€5,000–€50,000** |

## KfW 134 Terms (Genossenschaftsanteile)

**⚠️ MAJOR UPDATE: Significantly improved since 03.02.2026**

Source: KfW programme documentation, BMWSB press releases, aktion-pro-eigenheim.de

### Current Terms (since 03.02.2026)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Max loan per member | **€150,000** (was €100,000 before Feb 2026) |
| Tilgungszuschuss | **15%** (was 7.5% before Feb 2026) |
| Max grant amount | **€22,500** (15% × €150,000) |
| Laufzeit options | 4–25 years or 26–35 years |
| Zinsbindung | 5 or 10 years |
| Tilgungsfreie Anlaufzeit | 1–3 years (4–25yr) or 1–5 years (26–35yr) |

### Interest Rates (as of 03.02.2026 launch — subject to regular KfW adjustments)

All rates at launch were **well below 1%** across all durations and Zinsbindungen. Exact rates fluctuate; check KfW Konditionenanzeiger for current values.

- The programme applies to both new cooperative foundations (Neugründung) and acquisition of shares in existing cooperatives.
- The improved terms make KfW 134 substantially more attractive than modelled in the original document.

## KfW 297/298 Terms (Klimafreundlicher Neubau)

**⚠️ UPDATED: Significant rate changes since 02.03.2026**

### Current Terms (as of March 2026)

| Standard | Rate (eff. p.a., 10yr/10yr) | Max per WE |
|----------|-----------------------------|------------|
| EH40 (Klimafreundlicher Neubau) | **~0.60%** | €100,000 |
| EH40 + QNG | **~0.60%** | €150,000 |
| EH55 (befristet, since Dec 2025) | **~1.00%** | €100,000 |

- **Programme 297** = self-used residential (Selbstnutzer)
- **Programme 298** = rental/investor residential (Vermieter/Investoren) — applicable for cooperative lending
- Longer durations (25yr, 35yr) have higher rates; check KfW Konditionenanzeiger.
- No Tilgungszuschuss in these programmes — the subsidy is purely via rate reduction.
- The EH55 option is **time-limited** (befristet) and depends on available federal funds.
- As of 26.03.2026, some rates in 297/298 were increased again. Always check current conditions.

### Key Requirements
- No fossil-fuel heating (Öl, Gas excluded). Since March 2026 update: Biomasse is now permitted.
- Baugenehmigung must be in hand before application.
- An Energieeffizienz-Experte must be engaged for planning and Bestätigung zum Antrag (BzA).
- For QNG tier: additionally a Nachhaltigkeitsberater/Auditor is required.

### Notes for Calculator
- For a **cooperative building** (not individual ownership), **KfW 298** is the relevant programme number.
- The rate differential vs. market rates (~3.5–4.2% in early 2026) represents substantial savings: roughly **€2,000–3,000/year per €100k** in the first 10 years.
- Federal budget for KFN: €800M for 2025+2026 combined.

## Erbbauzins

**Default: 1.8% of Bodenrichtwert × land area per year**

### Verified Findings

- The Berlin Senate set the current Erbbauzins rates in **June 2021** (press release 23.06.2021):
  - **Geschosswohnungsbau** + soziale/kulturelle/sportliche Nutzung: **1.8%** of Bodenwert
  - Gewerbe/Industrie: 2.7%
  - Eigenheime/Eigentumswohnungen: 4.0%
- These rates replaced rates from 2018 and partially lowered them further.
- The **BIM Berliner Immobilienmanagement GmbH** administers Konzeptverfahren for city-owned land. In Konzeptverfahren, the Erbbauzins is typically part of the competitive offer (Wettbewerb), weighted at ~10%.
- **urban coop berlin eG** confirms the 1.8% rate for Direktvergaben but notes that in Konzeptverfahren, applicants may bid higher, which feeds through to cost rents.
- Comparison: Hamburg uses 1.3%; Köln 1.5% (with social housing conditions); Stuttgart and Lübeck 2.0%.

### Assessment

The 1.8% default is correct and current for Berlin Geschosswohnungsbau. In Konzeptverfahren, the effective rate may be higher due to competitive bidding.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Lower bound (discounted Konzeptverfahren) | 1.0% |
| Default / standard rate | 1.8% |
| Upper bound (competitive Konzeptverfahren bid) | 3.0% |
| **Slider range** | **0.5%–4.0%** |

## BGF-to-NUF Ratio

**Default: 1.30 · Range (slider): 1.15–1.50**

A ratio of 1.30 means 30% of gross floor area is "non-usable" (walls, stairs, hallways, elevator shafts, technical rooms). This is typical for a 4–5 storey multi-family building with one stairwell.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Lower bound (simple, no elevator, compact) | 1.15 |
| Median expected value | 1.28–1.32 |
| Upper bound (elevator, multiple stairwells, complex layout) | 1.45 |
| **Slider range** | **1.15–1.50** |

Note: No single standard source — varies by project. Architecture firms typically report ratios of 1.25–1.35 for standard MFH. The DIN 277 definitions of BGF vs. NUF apply.
